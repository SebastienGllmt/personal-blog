Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-01

This is the privacy policy for blog.sebastiengllmt.com (the "blog"), a personal blog operated by Sébastien Guillemot (the "operator"). It explains what data the blog collects, why, how long it is kept, and how to exercise your rights under the GDPR, the CCPA/CPRA, and the APPI.

Who is responsible

The data controller (GDPR) / business (CCPA) / Personal Information Handling Business Operator (APPI) for this blog is Sébastien Guillemot, based in Japan. The blog is run as a personal project, not as part of a commercial enterprise. For any privacy request, contact sebastiengllmt@gmail.com.

What the blog collects, and why

Engagement analytics

The blog writes a small set of anonymous engagement events to Cloudflare Analytics Engine, a server-side event sink the operator already uses as the data plane for this blog. Two kinds of events are recorded:

  • Page view — once per page load: the URL path you visited and the hostname of your referrer (the full referrer URL is never recorded). Same-origin referrers (clicking between pages on this blog) are omitted as uninformative.
  • Narration playback — only when you actually press play on a post's spoken track: a "play started" marker (with what triggered playback — the dock button, the Space key, an OS media key, or a chapter jump) and up to four "you crossed 25 % / 50 % / 75 % / 100 % of this talk" markers. Each is one row in the dataset; the markers fire at most once per page load and never if you don't press play. The post's master-track duration is stored alongside the play marker so completion is interpretable in absolute terms.

No cookies, no localStorage, no per-visitor identifier, no fingerprint. No IP address or other identifier is written to the analytics dataset — the operator never sees your IP in any query result, only the aggregate fields above. Cloudflare's edge still processes your request transiently (this is how every site fronted by Cloudflare works, not specific to analytics); refer to Cloudflare's own privacy policy for how Cloudflare handles request metadata at the edge. Known bots and crawlers are filtered before any event is written. Beacon delivery uses navigator.sendBeacon to a same-origin endpoint on this site — these engagement events post to the operator's own Worker, not to a third-party analytics origin.

Legal basis (GDPR): legitimate interest in understanding which posts get traffic and whether the spoken-narration feature is used, given the minimal, anonymous nature of the data collected and the absence of any profiling or cross-site tracking. CCPA: this is not a "sale" or "share" of personal information. APPI: data is anonymized and not retained in identifiable form by the operator. Row-level events age out per Cloudflare's standard Analytics Engine retention (approximately 3 months per their documentation).

Performance analytics (Core Web Vitals)

Separately from the engagement events above, this site has Cloudflare Web Analytics enabled. Cloudflare injects a small measurement script (beacon.min.js, served from static.cloudflareinsights.com) that runs in your browser and reports aggregate page-view counts and Core Web Vitals — real-world performance timings such as load speed, interactivity, and layout stability — back to Cloudflare. The operator uses this only to monitor whether pages load well for real visitors.

This beacon is cookieless: it sets no localStorage, no per-visitor identifier, and no fingerprint, and it does not track you across sites. It reports to Cloudflare — already this blog's hosting provider and sub-processor (see below) — so it introduces no new third party. Legal basis (GDPR): legitimate interest in operating a fast, reliable site, given the aggregate, anonymous nature of the data. You can block the beacon with any content blocker with no effect on the site.

Comments and login

If — and only if — you choose to leave a comment, you are asked to sign in via Google or Microsoft using OAuth 2.0. When you sign in, the blog receives, from your chosen provider:

  • your provider account ID (a stable identifier for your account at that provider);
  • your display name;
  • your email address;
  • your profile picture URL, if your provider returns one.

The blog uses your name and picture next to your comments, your email to reach you about replies, and your account ID to attribute future comments to the same person. Comments themselves (the text you write and the passage you anchor them to) are stored alongside that identity.

Where it is stored. Comments and the associated identity fields are stored in Cloudflare R2 object storage, served through a Cloudflare Worker. Cloudflare acts as a data processor / service provider on the operator's behalf.

How long. Comments are kept until you delete them or request deletion. The operator may delete comments at any time for moderation reasons (spam, abuse, off-topic).

Legal basis (GDPR): consent — by clicking "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Microsoft" and submitting a comment, you consent to the storage and display of your comment, name, picture, and the contact use of your email. You can withdraw consent at any time by deleting your comments or contacting the operator.

Cookies

The blog sets the following cookies, all of which are strictly necessary under the GDPR / ePrivacy framing and therefore do not require a cookie banner:

  • Session cookie (__Host-blog-session in production, blog-session in local development) — set only after you sign in to comment, holds a signed JWT identifying your account. HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Lax, lifetime 400 days. Cleared on sign-out.
  • OAuth flow cookies (blog-oauth-state-<provider>, blog-oauth-verifier-<provider>) — set briefly during the sign-in redirect to defend against CSRF and to complete PKCE. 10-minute lifetime, cleared on callback.

The blog does not set any analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking cookies. Cloudflare Web Analytics is explicitly cookieless.

Server logs

The Cloudflare Worker that serves this blog produces standard request logs (timestamp, method, path, response status, client IP at the edge) as a normal part of how Cloudflare's platform operates. These logs are used for debugging and to defend against abuse; the operator does not extract analytics from them, and Cloudflare's standard retention applies.

Service providers / sub-processors

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosting (Workers), object storage (R2), analytics (cookieless), edge security/CDN. See Cloudflare's privacy policy.
  • Google LLC — OAuth sign-in, only if you choose to sign in with Google. See Google's privacy policy.
  • Microsoft Corporation — OAuth sign-in, only if you choose to sign in with Microsoft. See Microsoft's privacy statement.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have some or all of the following rights:

  • Access — a copy of the personal information held about you. (GDPR Art. 15; CCPA §1798.110; APPI Art. 33)
  • Rectification / correction — fix inaccurate information. (GDPR Art. 16; CCPA §1798.106; APPI Art. 34)
  • Deletion / erasure — delete your comments and identity record. (GDPR Art. 17; CCPA §1798.105; APPI Art. 35)
  • Portability — receive your comments in a machine-readable format. (GDPR Art. 20; CCPA §1798.130; APPI Art. 33(5))
  • Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of past processing. (GDPR Art. 7(3))
  • Opt-out of sale or sharing — not applicable here because the operator does not sell or share personal information. (CCPA §1798.120)
  • Non-discrimination for exercising any of the above. (CCPA §1798.125)
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — for EU/UK residents, your national data protection authority; for California residents, the California Privacy Protection Agency; for residents of Japan, the Personal Information Protection Commission.

To exercise any of these rights, email sebastiengllmt@gmail.com from the address associated with your account. The operator will respond within 30 days (GDPR / APPI) or 45 days (CCPA), or notify you and explain the delay.

Children

The blog is not directed at children under 16, and the operator does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has signed in and posted a comment, contact the operator and the account and comments will be deleted.

Security

Session tokens are signed JWTs delivered in HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Lax cookies with the __Host- prefix in production. The blog enforces HTTPS site-wide, ships a CSP with no 'unsafe-inline' script sources, and sends standard hardening headers (HSTS, X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy, etc.). No system is perfectly secure; if you notice a security issue, please report it to the operator.
